Not Just an Ordinary
Story of Service

Over the past 30+ years as a teacher—from early chalkboard days to the modern classroom—this journey has been nothing short of extraordinary, filled with God’s grace and countless cherished moments. From first bells to final exams, early mornings to late evenings, my mother poured her heart into every lesson, every student, and every classroom she walked into. The staff, students, and fellow educators became more than colleagues—they became a family. Always supportive, always present, just a call or smile away. Her teaching was not just a profession, but a life of selfless service, with deep gratitude for the opportunity to shape young minds and touch countless lives. To the school, to the classroom, and to all the memories—you will be missed. It feels like the end of a long and beautiful chapter. But as we know, in teaching and in life, goodbyes are never truly the end.

“Teachers don’t raise voices, they raise generations.”

Handel

Biography

Esther Sanduki was born and brought up amidst five sisters and three brothers. The journey of leaving her hometown and moving to a new place was not easy, especially after her husband’s fatal accident. At one point, she even went to bed with an empty stomach, struggling financially. But when she embraced faith and believed in Jesus Christ as her Savior, blessings started pouring into her life. Within a short time, she secured a government job after having worked in the private sector for 9 years. Her life is a testament to perseverance and grace. She has dedicated 27+ years of service as a teacher, sharing her knowledge and compassion with students. Throughout her career, she has celebrated every event, dressed as Santa, giving chocolates, and praying for her students. Esther’s purpose at Riondong’a Comprehensive School has been fully realized, and her dedication continues to inspire all who know her. Let's celebrate her remarkable journey and selfless service together!
